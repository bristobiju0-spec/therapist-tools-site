# Best therapist directory sites: which ones actually send clients?

You’ve opened your private practice, polished your website, and… crickets. The biggest hurdle most therapists face isn’t clinical skill—it’s steady referrals. Directory sites promise visibility, but which ones put real clients on your calendar? I’ve tracked my own stats and polled colleagues to find out.

Below you’ll find the three directories that dominate therapist conversations: Psychology Today, Headway, and Alma. I’ll show you exactly what they cost, how many clients they actually send, and who should (and shouldn’t) list on each.

> **Quick verdict:** If you take insurance and practice in the US, Headway delivers the highest volume of referrals at zero marketing cost. Outside the US—or if you only take private pay—Psychology Today is still the only directory with global reach and consistent, if modest, referral flow.

### Pros

Therapists who want global reach and private-pay clients without worrying about insurance.

## FAQ

### Is Psychology Today worth it for UK therapists?
Yes—UK therapists report 1–3 new enquiries per month, and at £24.95 it remains the most trafficked UK-specific directory.

### How does Headway make money if it’s free?
Headway keeps the spread between what insurers pay and your contracted rate; you never pay a monthly fee.

### Can I list on both Headway and Alma?
Absolutely. Many therapists are paneled on both to keep schedules full; just track each platform’s fee structure.

### Which directory is best for Canadian therapists?
Psychology Today is the only viable option—Headway and Alma aren’t available in Canada yet.

### Do I need to be credentialed with insurers before joining Alma?
Yes, Alma requires active paneling with at least one major insurer in your state, but they assist with the paperwork.

If you’re outside the US, Psychology Today is still the only directory with reliable traffic. Inside the US, Headway sends the most insurance clients at zero marketing cost, while Alma adds peer support and faster payment for a small platform fee. Most busy therapists I know list on at least two of these platforms to keep caseloads steady.
