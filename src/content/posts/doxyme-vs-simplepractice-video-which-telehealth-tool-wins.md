# Doxy.me vs SimplePractice video: which telehealth tool wins?

You’ve got a full caseload, a 15-minute break between sessions, and a client who can’t figure out how to join the video room—again. If you’re running a solo practice, you don’t have an IT department to call. You need telehealth software that just works, keeps you HIPAA-compliant, and doesn’t eat into your already-thin margins. That’s why the doxy.me vs simplepractice video debate keeps popping up in therapist Facebook groups and hallway chats at conferences. I’ve used both platforms in my own practice, and I’ve helped colleagues migrate from one to the other. In this post I’ll walk you through the real-world pros, cons, pricing, and quirks so you can pick the one that actually fits your workflow.

> **Quick verdict:** If you only need rock-solid, no-frills video and a free tier, doxy.me wins. If you want video plus scheduling, billing, and a client portal in one subscription, SimplePractice’s integrated stack is worth the slightly steeper learning curve.

## Doxy.me: the dedicated telehealth workhorse

### Pricing
Doxy.me keeps the decision simple. There is a free plan that gives you unlimited 1:1 video sessions, a virtual waiting room, and basic screen sharing. When you outgrow the free tier, the first paid tier (Professional) is $29 per month. That adds group sessions, custom branding, and priority support. No annual contract is required; you can go month-to-month and cancel anytime. For solo therapists who bill hourly, one extra session pays for the whole month.

### Core features
- **Secure video conferencing** – peer-to-peer, HIPAA-compliant, no downloads for clients  
- **Screen sharing** – toggle on/off mid-session  
- **Virtual waiting room** – clients check in, you get a browser alert  
- **EHR integrations** – limited but growing list: TherapyNotes, SimplePractice, TheraNest, and a few others via HL7/API  
- **Group calls** – up to 10 participants on paid plans  
- **Custom branding** – add your logo and colors so the waiting room feels like your office  

### Pros
1. Zero client downloads. You text a link; they click; you’re in. That alone saves five minutes of “Do I need to download Zoom?” every session.  
2. Free tier is genuinely usable, not a 14-day teaser. I ran a part-time caseload of 12 clients on it for six months before upgrading.  
3. Video quality is crisp even on rural DSL. The platform adjusts bandwidth on the fly, so you don’t freeze mid-session when a client’s roommate starts streaming Netflix.  
4. Interface is spartan—no billing, no notes, no calendar—so you can’t accidentally click somewhere and leak PHI.  
5. Works in any modern browser on iPhone, Android, Windows, Mac, even a Chromebook.  

### Cons
1. No built-in scheduling. You’ll still need Acuity, Calendly, or your EHR to book appointments.  
2. No credit-card processing. If you charge no-shows, you’ll need Stripe, Square, or PayPal.  
3. Group sessions on the free plan are capped at two participants; you must upgrade for family therapy or group therapy.  
4. Reporting is minimal. You can download a CSV of session start/stop times, but that’s it.  
5. Customer support is email-only on the free tier; expect 24–48 hour turnaround.  

### Best for
Solo clinicians who already have an EHR or paper notes, want the lowest possible overhead, and value simplicity over bells and whistles. Perfect if you see 5–20 clients a week and don’t want another monthly subscription.

## SimplePractice video: the integrated practice suite

### Pricing
SimplePractice starts at $29 per month on the Starter plan (annual billing). That includes telehealth, calendar, client portal, and unlimited documentation. If you want insurance billing, automated ERA imports, and the full client portal with secure messaging, you’ll need the Plus plan at $59 per month. There’s also a Premium tier at $99 that adds advanced reporting and a mobile card reader. Annual discounts knock roughly 15 % off the monthly figures. No free tier exists, but you get a 30-day free trial that unlocks every feature.

### Core features
- **Secure video conferencing** – HIPAA-compliant, browser-based  
- **Calendar management** – color-coded, syncs to Google, Outlook, iCal  
- **Billing and insurance claims** – create CMS-1500s, submit electronically, auto-post ERAs  
- **Client portal** – intake forms, secure messaging, scheduling requests, payment on file  
- **Documentation** – treatment plans, progress notes, discharge summaries with templates  
- **Telehealth group sessions** – up to 12 participants  
- **Mobile app** – iOS and Android for both clinician and client  

### Pros
1. Everything lives in one login. You can schedule, conduct the session, write the note, charge the card, and submit the claim without ever leaving the platform.  
2. Client portal eliminates paper intake. Clients complete forms online; data auto-populates into their chart.  
3. AutoPay stores cards on file and charges after the session, cutting no-show revenue loss.  
4. Insurance features are robust: ERA imports show exactly what you were paid, and the system flags underpayments.  
5. Group practice friendly—if you expand, you can add interns or clinicians under the same umbrella account.  

### Cons
1. Steeper learning curve. Expect to spend a weekend watching tutorial videos and customizing note templates.  
2. Video module occasionally lags during peak hours (4 pm–8 pm EST). I’ve had to refresh the browser twice in the past year.  
3. Pricing jumps quickly. Starter plan doesn’t include ERA imports; most insurance-based therapists need the $59 tier.  
4. Mobile app is fine for calendar, but video sessions must be done on desktop browser for full functionality.  
5. No month-to-month discount; you pay the annual price up front to get the best rate.  

### Best for
Therapists who want an all-in-one solution: scheduling, notes, billing, and video under one roof. Ideal if you bill insurance, hate juggling multiple logins, or plan to scale into a group practice.

## FAQ

### Is doxy.me or SimplePractice video more reliable for back-to-back sessions?
Doxy.me edges out on raw reliability because its peer-to-peer architecture keeps working even if one user has shaky Wi-Fi. SimplePractice routes video through its servers to integrate with the calendar and notes, so peak traffic can cause brief stutters. In six months of daily use, I’ve had to reload SimplePractice twice; doxy.me never froze.

### Which platform is easier for elderly or tech-averse clients?
Both are browser-based, but doxy.me’s single-click room link is simpler. SimplePractice requires clients to log into the portal first, which can confuse users who forget passwords. If your caseload skews older, send a doxy.me link and save yourself tech-support calls.

### Can I bill insurance and take card payments through doxy.me?
Not directly. Doxy.me offers no billing module. You’ll need a separate merchant account (Stripe, Square) and then manually post payments to your EHR. SimplePractice includes card on file and auto-posts insurance payments from ERAs, so reconciliation takes minutes, not hours.

### How do security and HIPAA compliance compare between doxy.me vs SimplePractice video?
Both sign Business Associate Agreements and use end-to-end encryption. Doxy.me keeps video peer-to-peer, so session data never hits their servers. SimplePractice encrypts data at rest and in transit on their servers. Both have passed third-party audits; choose either and you’re covered.

### What happens if I outgrow my chosen platform—how painful is migration?
Doxy.me exports only session logs, so moving notes elsewhere is manual. SimplePractice lets you export client demographics, notes, and billing data in CCD format, which most EHRs can ingest. If you anticipate rapid growth, SimplePractice’s export flexibility makes future moves less painful.
