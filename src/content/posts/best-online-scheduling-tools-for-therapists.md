# Best online scheduling tools for therapists

You know the drill: a client emails at 11 pm asking for “any time next week,” you reply with three open slots, they pick one that’s already taken, and the ping-pong costs you two days. You just want a clean, HIPAA-safe way for clients to book themselves without the back-and-forth. This post walks you through the two online scheduling tools therapists actually use—SimplePractice and Calendly—so you can pick the one that matches how (and how much) you work.

> **Quick verdict:** SimplePractice is the all-in-one choice for therapists who want scheduling plus notes, billing and telehealth in a HIPAA system. Calendly is best for low-cost, lightweight booking that plugs into the tools you already use.

## FAQ

### Is online scheduling HIPAA compliant for therapists?
Yes, if the vendor signs a Business Associate Agreement. SimplePractice includes HIPAA on every paid plan; Calendly offers it on Essentials and above once you request the BAA.

### Can clients book themselves and still keep my calendar private?
Absolutely. Both tools let you set “available windows” so clients see only the slots you choose to offer; the rest of your calendar stays invisible.

### Do these tools send automatic reminders to reduce no-shows?
Yes. SimplePractice sends SMS/email reminders by default. Calendly does the same via Workflows once you’re on a paid plan.

### Will therapy appointment scheduling software sync with Google Calendar?
Yes. Both platforms offer 2-way sync with Google, and Calendly also supports Outlook and iCloud.

### Can I collect intake forms or payments at booking?
SimplePractice bundles intake forms and card-on-file in every plan. Calendly can attach Stripe payments and intake forms through its Workflows feature on paid tiers.
