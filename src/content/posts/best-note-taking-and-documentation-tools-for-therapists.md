# Best Note-Taking and Documentation Tools for Therapists

If you’ve ever ended a long clinical day staring at a blank progress-note template, you know the pain. You want to write concise, defensible, insurance-ready notes, but the tool you’re using either locks you into rigid boxes or leaves you with a blank page and zero guidance. Picking the right note-taking platform can mean the difference between closing your laptop at 6 p.m. or midnight. In this post I’ll walk you through three options I’ve used in my own solo practice—SimplePractice, TherapyNotes, and Notion—so you can decide which one actually fits your workflow, keeps you compliant, and doesn’t require a second mortgage.

> Quick verdict: TherapyNotes is the fastest way to produce compliant mental-health notes with almost zero setup, while SimplePractice gives you an all-in-one practice-management suite that includes scheduling and billing. Notion wins if you love tinkering and want a custom setup, but you’ll need to handle compliance yourself.

## SimplePractice – The Plug-and-Play EHR

SimplePractice markets itself as an all-in-one practice-management platform, and for most solo therapists that’s accurate out of the box. You get HIPAA-compliant note templates, integrated credit-card processing, a client portal, and telehealth that talks to the same calendar where you book appointments. Pricing runs $39–$179 per month depending on how many clinicians you add and whether you want the fully branded client portal. Every plan starts with a 14-day free trial; no credit card required until you subscribe.

Key features you’ll actually use:

- **Custom note templates**: Intake, SOAP, DAP, treatment-plan, and discharge summaries that auto-populate client details and pull forward your last treatment goals.
- **Automated billing**: Once you sign a note, the visit drops into a ready-to-submit claim with the correct CPT and modifier codes.
- **End-to-end encryption**: Data are encrypted in transit and at rest; SimplePractice will sign a Business Associate Agreement (BAA) at no extra cost.
- **Optional staff collaboration**: If you hire a virtual assistant or bring on a supervisor, you can grant role-based access for an extra fee.

Pros you’ll notice on day one:

- The interface feels modern and uncluttered. Buttons are where you expect them to be, so onboarding rarely takes more than an hour.
- Built-in EHR means you don’t need to export or copy-paste notes into another system when insurance requests records.
- The mobile app is genuinely useful for editing notes from your couch instead of logging into a desktop.

Cons you’ll notice after a few months:

- Template customization is limited. If you want nested checklists or outcome-measure scoring, you’ll hit walls quickly.
- Each additional staff seat raises the monthly cost, so group practices can get pricey.

Best for: Therapists who want the smallest learning curve and need HIPAA-compliant documentation, scheduling, and billing in one ecosystem.

## TherapyNotes – The Compliance Specialist

TherapyNotes was built by behavioral-health clinicians for behavioral-health clinicians. Every screen, drop-down, and report reminds you that the developers actually know what a “modifier 95” or a “GAF score” is. The platform has a free tier for students and new licensees; paid plans run $29–$79 per month and still undercut most medical EHRs. A 30-day free trial is standard, and the company is quick to extend it if you ask support.

Key features you’ll actually use:

- **Pre-written note blocks**: Click to insert phrases that satisfy Medicare, Medicaid, and most major commercial payers. The wording updates automatically when CPT codes change.
- **Telehealth integration**: One click launches a HIPAA-compliant video session, writes the date/time into the note, and stores a signed consent if needed.
- **Built-in billing codes**: CPT, ICD-10, and soon ICD-11 codes are updated quarterly; the claim scrubber flags common denials before you submit.
- **Patient intake forms**: Send a secure link; responses flow into the chart and auto-populate parts of your assessment.

Pros you’ll notice on day one:

- The audit trail is bullet-proof. Every login, note edit, and claim export is time-stamped, which saves hours if you’re ever subpoenaed.
- The free student license is full-featured, so you can finish practicum without paying.
- Customer support is U.S.-based and staffed by former billers who actually answer the phone.

Cons you’ll notice after a few months:

- The UI looks dated—think Windows 2010—and navigation can feel clunky if you’re used to slick SaaS apps.
- Premium templates and add-ons (e.g., outcome measures) cost extra, which can nickel-and-dime your budget.

Best for: Clinicians who want the fastest route to defensible, insurance-ready notes and who value compliance over aesthetics.

## Notion – The Creative Playground

Notion is a blank canvas that can become literally anything: a bullet journal, a project tracker, or a HIPAA-compliant note database if you set it up correctly. The base product is free for single users; paid plans with advanced permissions run $8–$18 per month. You won’t get scheduling or billing, but you will get relational databases, kanban boards, and markdown support that makes note-taking feel less like data entry and more like writing in a digital notebook.

Key features you’ll actually use:

- **Custom templates**: Build your own intake form, treatment-plan tracker, or DBT diary card with checkboxes, formulas, and embedded video links.
- **Database organization**: Link assessment scores to session notes, filter by diagnosis, and generate progress summaries in one click.
- **Role-based permissions**: Invite a supervisor or peer reviewer with read-only or comment-only access; revoke it later.
- **API integrations**: Sync with Google Calendar, Zapier, or even a HIPAA-compliant form tool like JotForm to automate intake.

Pros you’ll notice on day one:

- Unlimited customization. If you can dream up a workflow, you can build it.
- The free plan is generous—unlimited pages and 1,000 blocks is enough for a solo practice.
- Cross-platform sync means you can start a note on your phone and finish on your laptop offline.

Cons you’ll notice after a few months:

- There’s no built-in HIPAA certification; you must configure encryption, access logs, and sign a BAA with Notion yourself.
- You’ll need separate tools for scheduling, billing, and telehealth, which can fragment your tech stack.

Best for: Tech-savvy therapists who enjoy building systems and want complete control over how notes look and function.

## FAQ

**1. Do these tools support HIPAA-compliant note-taking for therapists in private practice?**  
Yes, but the responsibility level differs. SimplePractice and TherapyNotes both sign a Business Associate Agreement and maintain encryption at rest and in transit, so you’re compliant the moment you log in. Notion can be HIPAA-compliant, but you must configure your workspace correctly: disable public sharing, enable audit logs, limit access by role, and obtain a signed BAA from Notion. If you skip any step, you’re on the hook for the breach.

**2. Which note-taking tool is easiest for creating session notes quickly during back-to-back appointments?**  
TherapyNotes wins for speed. Its pre-written note blocks insert payer-specific language with one click, and the system auto-fills diagnosis, CPT codes, and session time from your calendar. SimplePractice is a close second with smart templates that pull forward treatment goals. Notion requires you to build or import templates first, so your first dozen notes take longer until your system is dialed in.

**3. Can I use these documentation platforms with telehealth video software?**  
SimplePractice and TherapyNotes include HIPAA-compliant video that launches directly from the chart; the session date and time stamp auto-populate your note. Notion doesn’t offer native telehealth, but you can embed a Loom, Google Meet, or Zoom link inside the client page. Remember to sign a BAA with the video vendor if you go that route.

**4. Are there free trials or free tiers available for solo therapists?**  
TherapyNotes offers a genuinely free tier for students and newly licensed clinicians with no time limit, though you’ll eventually need to upgrade to add billing. SimplePractice gives every user a 14-day free trial; you can extend it once by asking support. Notion’s free plan is unlimited for single users and generous enough to run a solo practice, but you’ll pay if you want advanced permissions or version history.

**5. How do pricing scales look if I expand into a group practice?**  
SimplePractice jumps from $39 for one clinician to $59 for two, then $179 for up to ten. TherapyNotes adds each clinician at $29 per month with no cap, so larger groups stay cheaper. Notion charges $8 per member per month on the lowest paid tier, making it affordable for small teams but lacking built-in EHR, so you’ll still need separate billing and scheduling tools.

## Conclusion

Choosing a note-taking platform ultimately comes down to how much setup you’re willing to do and whether you want extras like scheduling and billing bundled in. If you crave turnkey compliance and the fastest path to insurance-ready notes, TherapyNotes is hard to beat. If you want an all-in-one practice hub with modern design, SimplePractice delivers. And if you’re a builder at heart who loves granular control, Notion lets you craft a documentation system that matches your exact workflow—just be prepared to handle HIPAA and integrate your own billing. Whichever route you take, start with a free trial so you can click the buttons, write a few dummy notes, and see what feels intuitive after a long day of sessions.
