## QuickBooks: Best for Therapists Who Want Robust Reporting & Payroll

### Pricing
Simple Start runs $15/mo; after three months the promo ends and you’ll pay $35/mo for the Plus tier most therapists need for projects/classes.

### Stand-out features
- Therapy Profitability Report—break down revenue per CPT code so you can see whether 90834 or 90791 pays better.
- Bank feeds with AI categorization; set a rule once and every Stripe payout auto-splits between insurance and private-pay.
- Native payroll and 1099 e-filing if you ever hire a virtual assistant or associate clinician.

### Pros
- Industry-leading reporting (P&L by client, by modality).
- Seamless bank feeds + AI categorization.
- Payroll & 1099 e-filing built-in.
- CPA-preferred at year-end.

### Cons
- Steeper learning curve.
- Price hikes after promo period.
- HIPAA compliance requires manual BAA paperwork.

### Best for
Scaling solo therapists or group-practice owners who need payroll and granular KPIs. If you like dashboards and don’t mind spending a weekend setting them up, QuickBooks will grow with you.

## FreshBooks: Best for Time-Tracking & Simple Invoicing

### Pricing
Lite plan is $17/mo; you can upgrade to Plus ($30/mo) only when you need more than five active clients. 30-day free trial, no credit card required.

### Stand-out features
- Session-based invoicing with automatic late-payment reminders—clients click the email, enter a card, and you’re paid within 24 h (60% do, according to user feedback).
- Built-in time tracker links directly to invoices; great for couples or extended-session surcharges.
- Retainer and insurance superbill templates.

### Pros
- Fast, therapist-friendly invoicing with a session-notes field.
- Built-in time tracker links to invoices.
- Excellent live-chat support.
- Retainer/insurance superbill templates.

### Cons
- Limited chart-of-accounts depth.
- No native payroll (relies on Gusto).
- Reports less granular than QuickBooks.

### Best for
Therapists who want to send pretty invoices, accept online payments, and track session time without extra apps. If you hate bookkeeping but still want HIPAA compliance, FreshBooks is the easiest path.

## Who should choose which

- **Choose Wave** if you’re launching, money is tight, and you can manage HIPAA externally.  
- **Switch to FreshBooks** when monthly invoicing volume tops 20 or you want online payments without tech headaches.  
- **Move to QuickBooks** when you hire W-2 employees, need payroll, or want advanced reporting like revenue per CPT code.

## FAQ

### Is QuickBooks HIPAA-compliant for therapists?
Yes, but you must sign a Business Associate Agreement and avoid client names in memo fields—use initials or reference numbers only.

### Can I use Wave if I store client names?
Wave will not sign a BAA, so you should export data monthly and keep client identifiers out of the system. For strict HIPAA compliance, choose FreshBooks or QuickBooks.

### What is the best accounting software for a part-time private practice?
FreshBooks Lite gives you HIPAA compliance, time tracking, and online payments for $17/mo—cheaper than QuickBooks and easier than Wave’s manual work-arounds.

### How do I migrate from Wave to FreshBooks?
Export your Wave chart of accounts and general ledger as CSV files, then import into FreshBooks during the free 30-day trial; both support this via their support teams.

### Do any of these tools integrate with SimplePractice?
QuickBooks and FreshBooks both sync with SimplePractice; Wave does not. Use the integration to push session data and keep your EMR as the single source for PHI.
