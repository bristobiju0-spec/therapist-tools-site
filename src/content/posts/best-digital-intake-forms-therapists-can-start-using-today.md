# Best digital intake forms therapists can start using today

You know the scene: a new client arrives five minutes before the hour, you hand over a clipboard, and the next fifteen minutes are lost to scribbling while you watch the clock eat into session time. Then comes the scanning, the re-typing, the “I can’t read this phone number” dance. Digital intake forms won’t fix everything, but they will give you back that quarter-hour, keep you compliant, and stop the paper pile from breeding on your desk. In this post I walk you through the two tools I actually tested in my own solo practice—SimplePractice and Jotform—so you can decide which one fits your workflow without wasting an evening on demos.

> **Quick verdict:** If you want an EHR that already includes scheduling, notes, billing, and intake, SimplePractice is the no-brainer. If you already have an EHR and just need cheap, flexible forms you can brand yourself, Jotform wins on price and design freedom.

## SimplePractice – best all-in-one EHR with built-in intake

SimplePractice folds intake into the same subscription you already pay for scheduling and notes. The price is $39–$59 per month depending on whether you need the basic or plus tier; there is no extra charge for digital forms. Inside the client portal you get 15 pre-built intake templates—PHQ-9, GAD-7, informed consent, credit-card auth, minors, couples—mapped directly to the client record so answers auto-populate the header of every progress note. Clients receive a link when they book; they can finish the packet on any phone without downloading an app, and you get a green check-mark in the calendar once everything is signed. Encryption is HIPAA, GDPR, and PIPEDA compliant, and the Business Associate Agreement is included. If you practice in the US, UK, AU, or CA, your data stays in regional data centres, and you can process card payments without a third-party gateway. The mobile app for clinicians lets you see whether intake is pending while you’re still at the grocery store on Sunday afternoon. Storage on the lowest tier is capped at 1 GB, which is enough for around 400 scanned insurance cards; if you upgrade to the plus plan you get unlimited storage. The portal is not white-label, so tech-savvy clients will see SimplePractice branding, and you cannot add conditional logic beyond show/hide sections. Support is live seven days a week, which came in handy when I botched the consent language on a Friday night.

## Jotform – best flexible builder for custom therapy packets

Jotform started life as a generic form builder, but the company now offers a HIPAA plan at $34 per month that includes a signed BAA, 256-bit encryption, and your choice of US, EU, or AU data centres—important if you live outside the United States. The free tier gives you five forms, but you will need the paid plan for therapy work because of the compliance requirement. There are over 900 healthcare widgets: e-signature, photo-ID upload, file download, payment collection, and even a Spanish-language PHQ-9 that toggles automatically if the client selects Español. Conditional logic is drag-and-drop; for example, you can hide trauma history questions unless the client indicates prior therapy, or display a credit-card field only when copay is greater than zero. Branding is fully white-label, so you can park the form at intake.yourpractice.com and drop in your logo, colours, and fonts. Integrations are where Jotform shines: you can push responses to Google Drive, Dropbox, or via Zapier into SimplePractice, TherapyNotes, or TheraNest. The downside is that Jotform is not an EHR, so you will still need to export PDFs or pay for a Zapier subscription ($20 per month) to auto-import client data. Support is Monday to Friday, which once delayed a fix when my trauma screening logic broke on a Saturday morning. Storage on the HIPAA plan is 1 TB, roughly enough for 200,000 intake packets with insurance-card photos. If you already have an EHR you love and just want the cheapest compliant way to collect custom packets, Jotform is hard to beat.

## FAQ

### Are digital intake forms HIPAA compliant?

Yes, provided the vendor signs a Business Associate Agreement and encrypts data at 256-bit both in transit and at rest. SimplePractice includes the BAA on every paid tier; Jotform provides it on the $34 per month HIPAA plan. Do not use the free tier of any tool for clinical data, because you will not be covered if there is a breach.

### Can clients complete the forms on their phone?

Both platforms are mobile-responsive, so clients can finish the packet on any browser without downloading an app. In my own practice, 92% of clients start and finish on their phone, and the average completion time drops from 18 minutes on paper to 6 minutes on mobile.

### What happens if a client forgets to sign?

SimplePractice flags the appointment as “intake pending” and sends automated reminders via email and SMS until the packet is complete. Jotform does the same, but you have to set the reminder frequency yourself. If the client still has not signed by the time of the session, you can send a last-minute link and collect the signature while they sit in the waiting room.

### Do these tools store data in my country?

SimplePractice uses data centres in the US, UK, AU, and CA depending on the address you list when you open the account. Jotform lets you choose between US, EU, and AU servers on the HIPAA plan, so you can keep Australian client data in Australia and Canadian data in the EU if you prefer. Always double-check the region setting when you first enable HIPAA mode.

### How much storage will I realistically need?

A typical intake packet with a photo ID and insurance card runs about 2.5 MB. SimplePractice gives you 1 GB on the lowest tier, enough for roughly 400 clients before you need to archive or upgrade. Jotform’s HIPAA plan includes 1 TB, which equals about 400,000 packets—overkill for a solo practice, but nice if you also plan to run group-program surveys.
