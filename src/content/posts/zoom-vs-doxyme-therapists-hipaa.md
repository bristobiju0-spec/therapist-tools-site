You’ve been running sessions over FaceTime or the consumer Zoom plan because clients already know how to click the link. Then you realise—**this isn’t HIPAA-compliant** and you need a BAA *yesterday*. Now you’re stuck comparing Zoom for Healthcare vs Doxy.me, wondering which one won’t eat your whole budget or confuse clients.

This post walks you through the exact differences in pricing, compliance, and day-to-day use so you can pick one and get back to seeing clients.

> **Quick verdict:**  
Doxy.me Free gives you HIPAA-compliant 1:1 sessions at zero cost with no downloads; Zoom for Healthcare costs more but adds EHR hooks and group sessions. Pick the one that matches your cash-flow and feature list.

## Zoom for Healthcare features

- Business Associate Agreement (BAA) included on paid healthcare plans only  
- End-to-end encryption (must be enabled in settings)  
- HD video/audio with up to 500 participants  
- Waiting rooms, breakout rooms, session recording (stored under BAA)  
- Calendar and EHR integrations (Epic, SimplePractice, TherapyNotes, etc.)  
- Client must download the Zoom app on phones/tablets

## Zoom for Healthcare pros

- Clients already recognise the interface—fewer no-shows from tech panic  
- Video and audio quality stay crisp even on slower connections  
- Large meeting capacity if you run groups or webinars  
- Breakout rooms for couples or family work when you need separate sub-sessions  
- Cloud recording is BAA-compliant, so you can store session replays for supervision

## Zoom for Healthcare cons

- Only the paid healthcare plan is HIPAA-compliant; free or pro plans won’t sign a BAA  
- Mobile clients have to install the app—some workplace devices block downloads  
- Security toggles (waiting room, password, encryption) are buried in settings; one click missed = non-compliant session  
- Annual billing locks you in for the whole year even if caseload drops

## Zoom for Healthcare best for

Solo therapists who already use Zoom socially and want EHR integration, group sessions, or HD recording for supervision notes.

## FAQ

### Is Zoom free HIPAA-compliant for therapists?  
No. Only the paid Zoom for Healthcare plan signs a BAA and meets HIPAA requirements.

### Does Doxy.me free include a BAA?  
Yes. The free tier automatically includes a Business Associate Agreement.

### Can I do group therapy on Doxy.me free?  
No. Group sessions require a paid Doxy.me plan; free is 1:1 only.

### Which platform stores data in the US, and is that okay for UK therapists?  
Both store data in US-based AWS/Azure regions. UK therapists should sign the provided Data Processing Agreement and register with the ICO.

### Do clients need to create an account for either platform?  
No. Clients click your room link and enter their name—no sign-up required.
