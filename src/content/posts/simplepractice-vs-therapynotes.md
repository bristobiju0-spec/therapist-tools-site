You started your private practice to see clients, not to wrestle with software. Yet here you are, comparing two big names and still wondering which one will actually make your day easier. ## TherapyNotes Pricing

TherapyNotes starts at **$49/month** (US only) for up to **30 active clients**. Once you hit that ceiling, you move to the next tier—no unlimited option. Telehealth is **+$10/month** on every plan. A 30-day free trial is available and you can start without entering card details.

## SimplePractice Features

- Unlimited clients on every plan  
- Telehealth included (no extra plug-ins)  
- Mobile app that actually works—write notes on the tube or between sessions  
- Online booking with automated reminders  
- Stripe, Square, and PayPal payment processing  
- Basic billing; ERA/EDI available via add-ons  
- Localized for UK, AU, CA (currency, date format, GDPR-friendly)  

## TherapyNotes Features

- Mental-health-specific note templates (intake, intake update, termination, etc.)  
- Built-in ERA and EDI for US insurance—no third-party clearinghouse needed  
- Client portal for forms and secure messaging  
- Phone support included (you can actually speak to a human)  
- Telehealth module for +$10/month  
- Stripe integration only  
- US-only; no international compliance features  

## SimplePractice Pros

- Modern, clean interface—feels like an app, not a database  
- Telehealth is already paid for; one less line item on your P&L  
- Works if you relocate to the UK, AU, or CA—your data and settings move with you  
- Unlimited clients means no surprise upgrade emails  
- iOS/Android apps are full-featured, not watered-down versions  

## SimplePractice Cons

- No phone support—email and chat only, and replies can take a few hours  
- Note templates are generic; you’ll spend time customizing for mental health wording  
- Advanced US insurance tools cost extra; if you bill a lot of claims, price creeps up  

## TherapyNotes Pros

- Note templates built by clinicians—drop-down DSM cues, treatment-plan wizards  
- ERA/EDI included—most US claims process in 24-48 h without leaving the system  
- Phone support on every plan; call them when you’re staring at a rejected claim  
- 30-day trial with no card—easy to test-drive without fear of forgetting to cancel  

## TherapyNotes Cons

- Interface looks like 2010; lots of grey boxes and small text  
- Only works for US practices; no UK, AU, or CA compliance  
- Client limits on lower plans—30 may feel tight if you run groups or intensives  
- Telehealth costs extra; $10/month feels petty when you’re already at $49  

## Best for SimplePractice

Solo therapists outside the US or anyone who wants telehealth bundled and a modern UI that works on the phone in your pocket.

## Best for TherapyNotes

US-based solo practitioners who live and die by insurance billing and want mental-health-specific documentation without building templates from scratch.
