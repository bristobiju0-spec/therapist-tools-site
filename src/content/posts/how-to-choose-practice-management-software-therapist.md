# How to choose practice management software therapist

You left your agency job to stop wrestling with clunky systems, and now you’re the IT department. Pick the wrong practice-management platform and you’re back to nightly data-entry marathons, surprise billing fees, and explaining to clients why the portal won’t accept their post-code. This checklist walks you through the exact steps I used to narrow the field to two tools that actually fit solo therapy practices in the US, UK, AU and CA—then shows you which one wins on price, compliance and hassle factor.

> **Quick verdict:** SimplePractice gives you HIPAA/GDPR/PIPEDA-compliant video, unlimited clients and regional data hosting for under $70/mo wherever you practise; TherapyNotes is cheaper only if you’re US-based and don’t mind telehealth as a paid add-on.

## Step 1: List your must-have clinical features

Before you even open a browser, write down what you touch every day: note templates, DSM coding, treatment-plan autopopulate, e-sign, voice-to-text. If you do home visits, add “offline mobile app” to the list. These become your non-negotiables so you’re not swayed by shiny features you’ll never use.

## Step 2: Check licensing & data residency for US/UK/AU/CA

If you’re outside the US, insist on local servers. GDPR, PIPEDA and Australian OAIC guidance all require client data to stay in-region unless you have Model Contract Clauses—paperwork you don’t want to draft solo. SimplePractice hosts data in US-East, EU-West, AP-Sydney and CA-Central; TherapyNotes keeps everything in US-East only, so it’s off the table for UK/AU/CA therapists who need to stay compliant.

## Step 3: Evaluate integrated telehealth vs third-party add-ons

Zoom + HIPAA rider sounds fine until you’re paying an extra $20/mo and toggling between windows during a session. SimplePractice includes unlimited HIPAA-compliant video on the Essential tier and above. TherapyNotes charges $10/mo extra and still routes traffic through US servers—fine domestically, risky abroad.

## Step 4: Compare billing & insurance workflows

If you bill insurance, look for built-in ERA (electronic remittance advice) and a clearinghouse that covers your top five payers. SimplePractice connects to 2,700+ US payers and includes Stripe auto-billing; outside the US it plugs into GoCardless (UK), Medicare Easyclaims batch export (AU) and major Canadian insurers. TherapyNotes handles 8,000+ US payers but has zero support for non-US billing, so if you submit VAT, GST or provincial health you’ll be back to spreadsheets.

## Step 5: Test client portal & paperless intake

Send yourself a dummy intake form on your phone. Does it scroll cleanly? Does the client’s address autopopulate into the note? SimplePractice lets you customise every question and auto-maps answers into the chart. TherapyNotes has solid templates but they’re geared toward psychiatric practices—less flexibility for therapy-specific language.

## Step 6: Run total-cost math

Add up subscription, per-claim, SMS and telehealth surcharges. SimplePractice Essential is $69/mo (US), £59, AU$99 or CA$89 with unlimited claims and video. TherapyNotes is $49 for the first clinician, but add $10 for telehealth plus ~$0.18 clearinghouse fee per claim. At 25 claims/mo that’s still only ~$53.50—cheaper if you’re US-only and don’t need non-US data residency.

## Step 7: Audit support hours & onboarding

You’ll have questions at 9 pm when a client can’t log in. SimplePractice offers chat 7 am–6 pm Pacific and 24/7 email; you also get a 2-hour onboarding call. TherapyNotes gives you phone support until 8 pm ET and a 1-hour onboarding session. Both have active Facebook groups, but SimplePractice’s community is larger outside the US.

## Step 8: Export guarantee—can you leave with your data?

Read the fine print. SimplePractice retains your data for 90 days after cancellation and exports CSV, PDF, CCD and JSON. TherapyNotes keeps data 60 days and exports CSV, PDF and CCD only. Either way, plan a two-week overlap when you switch so you can migrate without a frantic last-day download.
