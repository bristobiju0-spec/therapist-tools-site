You’ve opened your practice, hung your shingle, and now you’re staring at two checkout pages: Owl Practice and SimplePractice. Both promise to run your calendar, billing, and notes, but one quotes you in CAD and stores data in Montreal while the other bills USD and keeps servers in Virginia. That difference alone can eat into your revenue and keep you up at night wondering if your college will flag you at audit.

In this post I’ll walk you through the exact comparison I wish I’d had when I chose my own EHR—pricing in real dollars, OHIP vs. manual billing, telehealth compliance with CASL/PIPEDA, and whether “unlimited clients” actually matters when you’re capped at 30 sessions a week.

**Quick verdict:**  
Owl Practice wins for Canadian therapists who need provincial billing and Canadian data residency without extra legwork. SimplePractice is the smoother, prettier tool if you’re cash-only or comfortable storing data in the US and handling taxes manually.

## Telehealth: CASL & PIPEDA compliance

## FAQ

### Is Owl Practice PIPEDA compliant for Canadian therapists?
Yes—servers and backups stay in AWS ca-central-1 (Montreal), so you meet PIPEDA and most provincial privacy acts without extra paperwork.

### Can SimplePractice submit OHIP e-claims?
Not at present. You can print CMS-1500 forms or manually enter claims in the MOH portal; reconciliation is done outside SimplePractice.

### Does Owl Practice generate T4A slips for CRA?
It auto-exports a T4A data file at year-end; you upload it to CRA’s online portal—no spreadsheet gymnastics.

### Which platform is cheaper after USD exchange?
At 1.35 FX, SimplePractice Basic with telehealth costs roughly $78 CAD vs. Owl’s $45 CAD solo plan.

### Do either integrate with Quebec’s RAMQ?
Neither platform offers direct RAMQ submission yet; Owl plans to add it in 2025.
